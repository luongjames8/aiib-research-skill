# Sourcing Methods — finding private companies

The toolkit for turning a sub-sector + a few known **anchors** into a ranked list of mostly-**private**,
mandate-fit companies. You don't find private operators by screening tickers — you start from something
known and **walk outward**. Use several complementary methods; the overlap between them is a signal a
name is real. Tag every candidate with the method + source that surfaced it (`provenance.md`).

## Anchors (your starting points)
From the sector scan (Mode A) you already have: listed sector leaders, DFI investees, and deal/auction
winners. If you have none, seed a few by searching "<sub-sector> <country> largest developers/operators".
Anchors are launch points, not the answer — the goal is the **private adjacents** around them.

## 1. Graph expansion (the core "company → companies" move)
From each anchor company, walk the graph to its:
- **Competitors** — other operators in the same sub-sector + country.
- **Suppliers & customers** — up/down the value chain (EPC contractors, component makers, offtakers).
- **JV partners & co-investors** — who builds/owns projects alongside it.
- **Fund-siblings** — the **other portfolio companies of its PE/DFI backers** (see method 2). This is the
  richest vein of private names.
Most of these adjacents are private. For each, capture: name, how it relates to the anchor, country/sub-sector.

## 2. Fund-following
Identify the **PE funds and DFIs active in this sub-sector** (IFC, ADB, FMO, BII/CDC, Proparco, DEG,
regional infra/PE funds, sovereign funds). **Enumerate their portfolio / investee companies** — these are
pre-screened for development-mandate fit and are overwhelmingly private. DFI disclosure portals
(esp. IFC) list investees by sector + country.

## 3. Value-chain decomposition
Map the sub-sector's value chain end-to-end, then hunt for a private player at **each link**. E.g.
utility-scale solar → project developers · IPPs/yieldcos · EPC · module/inverter suppliers · O&M · land
aggregators · financiers. Each link is a different company population; the obvious names are at one link only.

## 4. Source sweep (seed + cross-check)
- **Deal trackers + auction/tender winners** — DealStreetAsia, Tech in Asia, IJGlobal; sector tenders
  (SECI, PLN, EVN/DPPA, national auctions) name private sponsors/developers.
- **Regulatory / PPP pipelines** — Indonesia PPP book, Philippines PPP Center, India NIP, Brazil
  ANEEL/ANTT, Chile CNE/MOP — name private concession holders.
- **Credit ratings + bond issuance** — Pefindo / CRISIL / ICRA / Fitch new-issue & rating lists surface
  private companies of scale accessing capital now (and the rating reports double as a financials source).
- **News / M&A / IPO spillover** — recently funded or acquired private names.

## 5. Local-language mining
Search in **Bahasa, Vietnamese, Hindi, Thai, Portuguese, Spanish, Turkish** — the biggest unlock for the
private long tail that English-language results miss entirely. Translate the sub-sector + "developer /
operator / company" terms and search local business press, registries, and association member lists.

## Screening & ranking the candidates
- **Dedup** across methods (the same private name found three ways is a strong candidate).
- **Light mandate-fit gate** (`aiib-mandate.md`) — drop anything clearly outside AIIB's 6 sectors / 4
  themes. (Full mandate scoring happens later, in the dossier — keep this quick.)
- **Bias to PRIVATE / unlisted** operators — listed names are easy and usually already known; the value
  of this stage is the private pipeline.
- **Rank** by mandate fit + economic attractiveness of their sub-sector (from the Mode-A context) +
  signs of scale/fundability (rated, DFI-backed, recent raise).
- Output each with: name · sub-sector · listed/private · how-found (method + anchor) · one-line why ·
  provenance. This list feeds the company-dossier skill (Mode B).
