# AIIB Mandate Reference

Authoritative reference for AIIB's investment scope. Use this to judge **mandate fit**, NOT model
memory. Sourced from AIIB's public website (aiib.org), fetched 2026-06-22 — see **Sources** at the
bottom for the exact public pages. When citing mandate fit in output, tag it `[ref: aiib-mandate.md]`
and, where useful, link the relevant aiib.org page so the reader can verify on the public site.

**Mission:** "Financing Infrastructure for Tomorrow" (i4t) — sustainable infrastructure in Asia and
beyond. Multilateral development bank; AAA-rated; ~111 members.

## The 6 sectors AIIB invests in

Source: AIIB — "Our Work in Sectors"
(https://www.aiib.org/en/about-aiib/who-we-are/our-work-in-sectors/index.html).
A target company/project must map to at least one. The 6 official sectors plus the mandate's
"and other productive sectors" catch-all:

1. **Energy** — energy access & security, clean-energy transition, energy efficiency, local/regional
   pollution management, private-capital mobilization, connectivity & regional cooperation.
2. **Transport** — trade & economic growth, trunk linkages, cross-border connectivity, transport
   integration, upgrading existing infrastructure.
3. **Sustainable Cities** — urban development, urban mobility, basic infrastructure & urban resilience,
   integrated development, freestanding health & education facilities. (This is AIIB's "urban" sector —
   includes urban water supply, sanitation, social infrastructure.)
4. **Digital Infrastructure** — hard digital infrastructure (data centers, fiber, towers, connectivity)
   AND technology adoption across traditional infrastructure + other productive sectors.
5. **Water** — access to water services, water availability/quality via better resource management,
   resilience to water-related disasters, innovative water technologies. (Incl. WtE/wastewater.)
6. **Health** — health co-benefits across infrastructure, the health-service value chain, health
   security, green/climate-resilient health systems, technology-based health outcomes, health financing.

**"...and other productive sectors"** — the mandate explicitly extends beyond the 6 (agri-logistics,
manufacturing-adjacent, financial-institution intermediation/funds). Use when a company is clearly
mandate-aligned via a theme below but doesn't sit squarely in one of the 6.

## The 4 thematic priorities (Corporate Strategy, updated 2025)

Source: AIIB — "Our Thematic Priorities"
(https://www.aiib.org/en/about-aiib/who-we-are/our-thematic-priorities/index.html).
Cross-cutting lenses; strong fit usually hits ≥1:

1. **Green Infrastructure** — local environmental improvement + climate mitigation/adaptation. All AIIB
   financings since July 2023 are Paris-Agreement-aligned.
2. **Connectivity and Regional Cooperation** — domestic & cross-border infrastructure connectivity;
   regional trade, investment, digital & financial integration.
3. **Technology-enabled Infrastructure** — technology that delivers better value, quality, productivity,
   efficiency, resilience, sustainability, inclusion, transparency, or governance across the life cycle.
4. **Private Capital Mobilization** — projects that mobilize private financing into mandate sectors;
   leading transactions, actively crowding in the private sector.

## 2030 strategy targets (use as directional anchors)

Source: AIIB — "Corporate Strategy"
(https://www.aiib.org/en/about-aiib/who-we-are/corporate-strategy/index.html).

- Double annual financing to ~**USD 17 billion/yr** by 2030.
- **>50%** of own financing to climate-related investments annually; total climate finance > USD 50bn.
- **25–30%** of financing to cross-border connectivity projects.

## How to score mandate fit (output the verdict, cite this file)

- **Sector match** — which of the 6 (or "other productive sectors") + one-line why.
- **Theme match** — which of the 4 thematic priorities, with the specific reason.
- **Climate/Paris** — green or climate-aligned? (matters given >50% climate target).
- **Verdict** — `Strong fit` / `Partial fit` / `Out of mandate`, with the bear case.
